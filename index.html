•Brief History of Snooker

It has been over 140 years since the sport was invented. Now it is very popular in the UK, China, Thailand and other countries. At the 2019 World Championship, the best players on the planet competed for a top prize of £500,000 and the total prize pool was over £2 million, watched by half a billion people around the globe. It's hard to imagine how Snooker deveploed from nothing.


 India, the birthplace

Now most of professional snooker opens or championships take place in the UK. You might think snooker is orignated from the UK. Acutually, you are wrong. The game of snooker was invented in 1875 by the British Army in the Indian town of Jubbulpore. So, India is the real birthplace of snooker. It is recorded that young lieutenant Neville Chamberlain was experimenting on the officers’ mess table with the existing game of ‘Black Pool’ featuring 15 red balls and a black.


 Snooker, a slang term for rookie

Having thrown on additional coloured balls and heard that rookie cadets at the Royal Military Academy at Woolwich were called ‘snookers’, Chamberlain observed that all those present were snookers at this tweaked version of the game, and the name instantly stuck.


Afterwards, the game spread to other Indian colonies and the UK. After decades of development, it has become one of the most attractive sports games. Now it is governed by the World Professional Billiards and Snooker Association (WPBSA).

•Triple Crown Series

 The World Championship

The World Snooker Championship is the leading snooker tournament both in terms of prestige and prize money. It has been played at the Crucible Theatre in Sheffield, England since 1977. It is the toughest snooker tournament. If you want to win, you have to win 18 out of 35 frames in the final. The reigning champion is Judd Trump.

 The UK Championship

UK Championship is the second-biggest ranking tournament, after the World Championship. It has been staged at the Barbican Center, York, England since 2011. It's also very tough to win. The winner needs to continuely win from first round to fourth round, then quarter-final, semi-final. All these matches are 11 frames and the winner needs to win 10 out of 19 frames in the final. Last year the total prize money was over £1 million and the winner received £200,000. The reigning champion is Ding Junhui.

 The Masters

Unlike the other two ranking event, the Masters is an invitation event. Only the elite top 16 players are invited to compete for the famous trophy and a top prize of £250,000. It has been staged at the iconic Alexandra Palace in North London since 2012. The Masters trophy is named in honor of Paul Hunter, who won three times in four years in the Masters and he died from cancer at the age of 27. The reigning champion is Stuart Bingham.
