Current professional snooker world, there are serveral elite players who dominate snooker champions. These players are ranked world top 10 all the years. They are unbeatable for some players. Each one has different potting styles. Some are good at defending while some have high potting success so they can play more aggressively. The difference between these players and others is that their snooker skills are very comprehensive. Those who are good at defending doesn't mean they would lose the pots that they think should have potted in. Also, they have very steady state of mind, they know the winning mentality. That's why they can stand in the stage of world top 10. Let me introduce several of them. Any snooker fan is familiar with them.

•The Masters

 Judd Trump: The Aces

Judd Trump, current World No.1, English player. He has stunning long pot success rate and very strong cue power. So his performance gets a lot of appreciation from the audience. He made 147 maximun break in the formal match when he was only 14. He won 6 ranking titles in a single season, breaking the record. He has passed 700 century breaks in professional competiton, a milestone that only 5 players have reached. Most importantly, he has won all the three Triple Crown events at one time. He is only 30 years old. He has realized all the dreams that other professional players spend the whole career time chasing for.

Judd Trump

 Mark Williams: Welsh Potting Machine

Mark Williams, born in 1975, current World No.3, Welsh player. Three-time World Championship, two-time UK Championships, two-time Masters. He has won 22 ranking tournaments overall. He is the first left-handed player to win the World Championship. The fans then give him the nickname "Golden Left Hand". He loves to show his ability of potting, such as eye-closed shot or without watching the cue ball. He has two 147 maximun breaks and 486 century breaks.

Mark Williams

 John Higgins: The Wizard

John Higgins, born in 1975, current World No.4, Scottish player. Four-time World Championship, three-time UK Championships, two-time Masters. He has won 30 ranking titles and got nine times 147 maximun break, making him one of the greatest players in snooker history. His potting style may not as marvelous as Judd Trump and Mark Williams, but his thought of clearance and skills can be written into snooker textbook. He has compiled more than 750 century breaks in professional tournament play, second only to O'Sullivan.

John Higgins

 Ronnie O'Sullivan: The Rocket

Ronnie O'Sullivan, born in 1975, current World No.6, English player. Five-time World Championship, seven-time UK Championship, seven-time Masters. He holds the record total of 19 titles in Triple Crown events. He shares the record for the most ranking titles (36) with "snooker emperor" Stephen Hendry. He is the only player to have reached 1,000 century breaks. He plays snooker so fast that the referee needs to hurry to respot the colored balls. So the fans give him the nickname "The Rocket". He finished 147 maximun break at the 1997 World Championship in just 5 minutes and 8 seconds. He has stunning 15 times 147 maximun break in professional competition, the highest among players. It's a great pleasure to watch him play snooker. You would get a thinking that snooker is very easy.

Ronnie O'Sullivan

 Mark Selby: The Jester

Mark Selby, current World No.7, English player. Three-time World Championship, two-time UK Championships, three-time Masters. He was the sixth player to win all of snooker's Triple Crown events at least twice, which is the highest achievements among young players born in 1980s. He was number one in the world rankings for over four years continuously. He is really good at defending and making snooker. He has won 17 ranking titles, same as Judd Trump. He is a hardworking player. You often see him practicing in the club.

Mark Selby

 Ding Junhui: The Dragon

Ding Junhui, current World No.10, Chinese player. He has won three UK Championships and one Masters. He has made more than 500 century breaks, including 6 maximun break. He has won 14 ranking titles. He was the first and only Asian palyer to be ranked world No.1, making him one of the greatest Asian players in snooker history. He is good at controlling cue ball postion, so he can pot the next ball easily and make high breaks.

Ding Junhui
